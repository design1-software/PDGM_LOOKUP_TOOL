# Documentation Roadmap Reference - Ongoing Visit Documentation

## CRITICAL MEDICARE FRAMEWORK FOR ONGOING VISITS

**Roadmap Documentation Purpose:**
- **Post-SOC Visit Documentation** - Ongoing skilled visits after initial OASIS completed
- **Discipline-Specific Responsibilities** - RN, PT, OT, SLP visit documentation requirements
- **PDGM-Specific Assessment Priorities** - Focus areas based on PDGM grouping characteristics
- **Skilled Need Continuation** - Justifying ongoing skilled care between OASIS assessments

**ANTI-HALLUCINATION GUARDRAILS:**
- Use ONLY evidence-based clinical assessment findings
- Reference ONLY verified skilled care interventions per Medicare guidelines
- Include ONLY realistic patient responses to treatment
- Demonstrate ONLY appropriate discipline-specific scope of practice
- Never fabricate clinical findings or patient progress

## RN ONGOING VISIT DOCUMENTATION

### COPD Management - Post-SOC Visits (J44.1)

**Visit 3 - Day 7 Post-SOC**

**Respiratory Assessment:**
*Clinical Findings: Breath sounds clear bilaterally, no adventitious sounds. SpO2 94% on room air at rest, 91% with ambulation 20 feet. Respiratory rate 18 at rest, 24 with minimal exertion. Patient demonstrates improved inhaler technique with spacer device.*

*Skilled Intervention: Assessed respiratory status, monitored oxygen saturation with activity. Reinforced proper inhaler technique and spacer use. Educated on pursed-lip breathing technique for dyspnea management.*

*Patient Response: Verbalizes understanding of breathing techniques. Demonstrates correct inhaler use with return demonstration. Reports decreased anxiety about breathing since learning techniques.*

*Clinical Rationale: Skilled nursing assessment required to monitor respiratory status post-exacerbation and ensure medication effectiveness. Patient education on breathing techniques requires skilled nursing judgment.*

**Medication Management:**
*Assessment: Reviewed medication list with patient. Prednisone taper progressing as ordered (currently 20mg daily, day 7 of taper). Patient reports no side effects from steroid therapy. Blood glucose monitoring shows readings 140-160 mg/dL.*

*Skilled Intervention: Medication reconciliation completed. Educated on steroid taper importance and side effect monitoring. Reinforced blood glucose monitoring due to steroid-induced hyperglycemia.*

*Documentation: Patient demonstrates understanding of medication schedule. Blood glucose log reviewed and found accurate. No medication discrepancies identified.*

**Visit 6 - Day 14 Post-SOC**

**Progress Assessment:**
*Clinical Findings: Significant improvement in activity tolerance. Patient ambulates 50 feet with walker before mild dyspnea. SpO2 maintains 93-95% with activity. Breath sounds remain clear. Completed prednisone taper without complications.*

*Skilled Assessment: Evaluated response to treatment plan. Assessed for signs of COPD exacerbation recurrence. Monitored for steroid withdrawal effects.*

*Patient Education: Reinforced recognition of early exacerbation signs (increased dyspnea, sputum color change, decreased activity tolerance). Reviewed action plan for symptom management.*

*Care Plan Modification: Reduced visit frequency from 2x/week to 1x/week based on clinical stability and improved self-management.*

### CHF Management - Post-SOC Visits (I50.21)

**Visit 4 - Day 10 Post-SOC**

**Cardiovascular Assessment:**
*Clinical Findings: Daily weights stable (±1 lb variation). Lower extremity edema decreased from 2+ to 1+ bilaterally. Lung sounds clear, no S3 gallop. Blood pressure 128/78, heart rate 72 regular.*

*Skilled Intervention: Assessed fluid status and response to diuretic therapy. Reviewed daily weight log with patient. Evaluated medication adherence and effectiveness.*

*Patient Response: Demonstrates accurate daily weight technique. Verbalizes understanding of fluid restriction (2L daily). Reports improved energy and decreased shortness of breath.*

*Clinical Rationale: Skilled nursing assessment required to monitor CHF stability and prevent rehospitalization. Complex medication regimen requires ongoing skilled monitoring.*

**Medication Review:**
*Assessment: Lasix 40mg daily well-tolerated. Potassium level 4.2 mEq/L (within normal range). ACE inhibitor continued without side effects. Patient adherent to medication regimen.*

*Skilled Intervention: Reviewed medication schedule and importance of adherence. Educated on signs of electrolyte imbalance. Coordinated with physician regarding stable clinical status.*

*Documentation: No medication discrepancies. Patient demonstrates understanding of when to contact physician (weight gain >2 lbs in 24 hours, increased dyspnea).*

## SKILLED NEED JUSTIFICATION FOR ONGOING VISITS

### Complexity Factors Requiring Skilled Care

**Clinical Instability:**
- Fluctuating vital signs requiring skilled assessment
- Medication adjustments based on clinical response
- Risk of complications requiring skilled monitoring
- Complex symptom management requiring nursing judgment

**Patient Education Needs:**
- Complex medication regimens requiring skilled teaching
- Disease management requiring skilled assessment of understanding
- Safety education requiring skilled evaluation of competency
- Caregiver training requiring skilled assessment of capability

**Functional Limitations:**
- Progressive functional decline requiring skilled intervention
- Safety risks requiring skilled assessment and intervention
- Equipment needs requiring skilled evaluation and training
- Environmental modifications requiring skilled assessment

### Documentation Requirements for Continued Skilled Need

**Objective Clinical Findings:**
- Specific vital signs, assessment findings, and measurements
- Functional performance data with objective criteria
- Patient response to interventions with measurable outcomes
- Progress toward goals with quantifiable improvements

**Skilled Intervention Justification:**
- Clinical judgment required for assessment and intervention
- Complexity of care requiring professional skills
- Patient education requiring skilled assessment of learning
- Safety risks requiring skilled monitoring and intervention

**Patient Progress Documentation:**
- Measurable improvements in functional status
- Achievement of short-term goals leading to long-term outcomes
- Modification of interventions based on patient response
- Continued need for skilled services despite progress

## SAMPLE VISIT DOCUMENTATION INTEGRATION

### Comprehensive RN Visit Note Example

**Patient:** 74-year-old female with COPD exacerbation (J44.1)
**Visit:** Day 10 post-SOC, skilled nursing visit 4 of 12 planned

**Skilled Assessment:**
Comprehensive respiratory assessment completed. Breath sounds clear bilaterally with good air movement. Respiratory rate 16 at rest, 22 with ambulation 30 feet. SpO2 95% on room air at rest, 92% with activity (improved from 88% at SOC). Patient demonstrates correct inhaler technique with spacer device and verbalizes understanding of medication schedule.

**Skilled Intervention:**
Medication reconciliation completed with no discrepancies identified. Reinforced importance of prednisone taper completion and monitoring for side effects. Educated on recognition of early exacerbation signs including increased dyspnea, sputum color change, and decreased activity tolerance. Reviewed emergency action plan with patient and caregiver.

**Patient Response:**
Patient demonstrates improved understanding of COPD management. Correctly identifies three signs of exacerbation and appropriate actions to take. Shows increased confidence with self-care activities and reports decreased anxiety about breathing. Caregiver verbalizes understanding of when to contact physician or home health agency.

**Clinical Progress:**
Significant improvement in respiratory status since SOC. Activity tolerance increased from 20 feet to 30 feet ambulation without severe dyspnea. Patient demonstrates independence with medication management and inhaler technique. Stable clinical status supports reduction in visit frequency from 2x/week to 1x/week.

**Plan:**
Continue skilled nursing visits 1x/week for 2 weeks to monitor continued stability and reinforce self-management skills. Patient education ongoing regarding COPD management and exacerbation prevention. Coordinate with physician regarding stable clinical status and medication effectiveness.

This documentation demonstrates skilled need through complex assessment requirements, patient education needs, and ongoing monitoring for clinical stability while showing measurable progress toward independence.
