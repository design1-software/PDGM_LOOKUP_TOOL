## OFFICIAL OASIS-E ASSESSMENT SECTIONS

### Section A: Administrative Information

**M0100: This Assessment is Currently Being Completed for the Following Reason**
*Intent: Identify the reason the assessment is being completed*
*Assessment: Administrative determination based on episode timing*
*Coding Options:*
- 1 = Start of care—further visits planned
- 3 = Resumption of care (after inpatient stay)  
- 4 = Recertification (follow-up) assessment
- 5 = Other follow-up assessment
- 6 = Transferred to an inpatient facility—patient not discharged from agency
- 7 = Transferred to an inpatient facility—patient discharged from agency
- 8 = Death at home
- 9 = Discharge from agency

### Section B: Patient History and Diagnoses

**M1021: Primary Diagnosis**
*Intent: Identify the primary diagnosis for this home health episode*
*Assessment: Review physician orders and medical records*
*Coding: Enter the ICD-10-CM diagnosis code that represents the primary reason for home health services*
*Clinical Guidance: Primary diagnosis should be most related to current plan of care*

**M1023: Other Diagnoses**
*Intent: Identify all other diagnoses for the patient that require changed treatment, increased services, or present a safety hazard*
*Assessment: Review complete medical history and current conditions*
*Coding: List up to 5 additional ICD-10-CM diagnosis codes*
*Documentation Requirements: Include only diagnoses impacting home health care*

**M1033: Risk for Hospitalization**
*Intent: Identify if the patient is at risk for hospitalization*
*Assessment: Clinical judgment based on patient condition and support systems*
*Coding Options:*
- 0 = No
- 1 = Yes
*Assessment Factors: Clinical instability, medication complexity, caregiver support*

### Section C: Sensory Status

**C1310: Signs and Symptoms of Delirium (Inattention, Disorganized Thinking, Altered Level of Consciousness, Psychomotor Agitation)**
*Intent: Identify signs and symptoms of delirium*
*Assessment: Direct observation during assessment period*
*Coding Options:*
- 0 = Behavior not present
- 1 = Behavior continuously present, does not fluctuate
- 2 = Behavior present, fluctuates (comes and goes, changes in severity)

### Section GG: Functional Abilities and Goals

**GG0130A: Eating**
*Intent: Identify the patient's ability to use suitable utensils to bring food and/or liquid to the mouth and swallow food/liquid once the meal is placed before the patient*
*Assessment: Observation and demonstration preferred over 3-day assessment period*
*Coding Options:*
- 01 = Independent - Patient completes the activity by themselves with no assistance from a helper
- 02 = Setup or clean-up assistance - Helper sets up or cleans up; patient completes activity
- 03 = Supervision or touching assistance - Helper provides verbal cues and/or touching/steadying assistance
- 04 = Partial/moderate assistance - Helper does LESS THAN HALF the effort
- 05 = Substantial/maximal assistance - Helper does MORE THAN HALF the effort
- 06 = Total dependence - Helper does ALL of the effort
- 07 = Activity was not attempted due to environmental limitations or safety concerns

**GG0130B: Oral Hygiene**
*Intent: Identify the patient's ability to use suitable items to clean teeth, dentures, and mouth*
*Assessment: Observation of patient's typical performance*
*Coding: Use same scale as GG0130A (01-07)*

**GG0170A: Roll Left and Right**
*Intent: Identify the patient's ability to roll from lying on back to left and right side, and return to lying on back on the bed*
*Assessment: Observation of bed mobility over assessment period*
*Coding: Use same scale as GG0130A (01-07)*
*Clinical Significance: Bed mobility impacts repositioning and pressure ulcer prevention*

**GG0170C: Sit to Stand**
*Intent: Identify the patient's ability to come to a standing position from sitting in a chair or on the side of the bed*
*Assessment: Observation of transfer ability*
*Coding: Use same scale as GG0130A (01-07)*
*Safety Considerations: Fall risk and need for assistive devices*

### Section J: Health Conditions

**J1100: Pain Assessment**
*Intent: Identify if the patient has pain and the severity of pain*
*Assessment: Patient self-report with clinical validation*
*Coding Options:*
- 0 = No pain
- 1 = Pain present but not daily
- 2 = Daily pain
*Documentation: Include pain management strategies and effectiveness*

### Section M: Skin Conditions

**M1311: Pressure Ulcer Assessment**
*Intent: Identify the number of pressure ulcers at each stage*
*Assessment: RN assessment using NPUAP staging guidelines*
*Coding Format:*
- A1 = Number of Stage 2 pressure ulcers
- B1 = Number of Stage 3 pressure ulcers  
- C1 = Number of Stage 4 pressure ulcers
- D1 = Number of unstageable pressure ulcers
*Assessment Requirements: Complete skin assessment with staging rationale*
