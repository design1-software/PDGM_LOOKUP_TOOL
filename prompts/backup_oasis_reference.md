# Sample OASIS Assessment Reference

## Example: J44.1 - COPD with Acute Exacerbation

### Patient Demographics
**Name:** Jane Doe  
**DOB:** 03/25/1950 (74 years old)  
**Gender:** Female  
**Living Situation:** Resides with husband in single-story home with 3 steps to entry

### Clinical Status
**Primary Diagnosis:** J44.1 – COPD with (acute) exacerbation  
**Secondary Diagnoses:** I10 – Hypertension; E11.9 – Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus  
**Current Health Conditions:** Increased SOB, wheezing, cough with yellow sputum, SpO2 88% on room air, mild pedal edema  
**Recent Hospitalization:** Discharged from Memorial Hospital 3 days ago for COPD exacerbation  
**Current Medications:**
- Albuterol/ipratropium inhaler QID
- Prednisone 40mg daily x5 days
- Lisinopril 20mg daily
- Metformin 500mg BID

### Functional Status
**Bathing:** Needs assistance for shower transfer due to SOB and fatigue  
**Dressing:** Requires extra time; independent with adaptive techniques  
**Ambulation:** Limited to 20 feet with walker before dyspnea  
**Toileting/Eating:** Independent  
**Mobility Devices:** Uses 4-wheel walker, no recent falls

### Psychosocial Status
**Cognition:** Alert, oriented x4  
**Mood:** Reports mild anxiety about breathing; denies depression  
**Support System:** Husband is primary caregiver; daughter visits weekly

### Health Service Utilization
**Recent Hospitalizations:** 1 in last 30 days (COPD exacerbation)  
**ED Visits:** 0 in last 30 days  
**Home Health Visits:** SOC, skilled nursing 2x/week planned; PT evaluation pending

### Home Environment & Caregiver Information
**Home:** Clean, smoke-free, all necessities on main floor  
**Caregiver:** Husband, reliable; aware of care plan and signs of exacerbation  
**Barriers:** 3 entry steps, otherwise safe for mobility

### Skin Conditions
**Assessment:** Intact; no pressure ulcers, wounds, or breakdown

### Swallowing/Nutritional Status
**Swallowing:** No difficulties; denies choking or coughing when eating  
**Nutrition:** Eating well, balanced diet; reports weight loss of 3 lbs during hospitalization

### Bladder and Bowel Function
**Bladder:** Continence intact, no catheter  
**Bowel:** Regular; no incontinence or constipation

### Prior Functioning and Device Use
**Prior to Exacerbation:** Independent with ADLs, ambulated without device  
**Current:** Needs walker for ambulation, limited endurance

### Drug Regimen Review
**Assessment:** Medications reviewed; no discrepancies  
**Teaching:** Patient and caregiver educated on proper inhaler/nebulizer use, prednisone taper, side effects, and signs of adverse drug reactions  
**Issues Identified:** None at this time; plans for weekly med reconciliation

### Plan of Care/Interventions
**Skilled Nursing:** Assess respiratory status, monitor SpO2, med reconciliation, teach on inhaler/nebulizer use, exacerbation signs, and oxygen safety  
**Physical Therapy:** Evaluate/restore functional endurance, gait training, energy conservation  
**Caregiver Teaching:** Recognize early exacerbation signs, when to call MD/home health, safe transfer techniques  
**Frequency:** SN 2x/week x3 weeks, PT eval/treat 2x/week x2 weeks, may adjust based on progress

### Summary/Goals
**Short-term:** Maintain SpO2 >90% on room air or as ordered, remain free from hospitalization, demonstrate correct inhaler use, safely ambulate 30+ feet with walker  
**Long-term:** Return to baseline function, reduce anxiety, manage COPD symptoms at home, prevent complications
