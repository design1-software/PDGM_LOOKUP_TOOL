## OASIS-E ASSESSMENT STRUCTURE

### Section A: Administrative Information
**M0100: This Assessment is Currently Being Completed for the Following Reason**
- Intent: Identify the reason the assessment is being completed
- Coding Options: 1=Start of care, 3=Resumption of care, 4=Recertification, etc.
- Documentation Focus: Proper timepoint identification and assessment timing

### Section B: Patient History and Diagnoses  
**M1021: Primary Diagnosis**
- Intent: Identify the primary diagnosis for this home health episode
- Assessment Method: Review physician orders and medical records
- Documentation Requirements: ICD-10-CM code most related to current plan of care

**M1023: Other Diagnoses**
- Intent: Identify all other diagnoses requiring changed treatment or presenting safety hazards
- Coding: Up to 5 additional ICD-10-CM codes
- Clinical Considerations: Impact on care planning and resource utilization

**M1033: Risk for Hospitalization**
- Intent: Identify if patient is at risk for hospitalization
- Coding Options: 0=No, 1=Yes
- Assessment Factors: Clinical instability, medication management, support systems

### Section C: Sensory Status
**C1310: Signs and Symptoms of Delirium**
- Intent: Identify signs and symptoms of delirium
- Coding: 0=Not present, 1=Continuously present, 2=Present and fluctuates
- Assessment Method: Direct observation and caregiver interview

### Section GG: Functional Abilities and Goals
**GG0130A: Eating**
- Intent: Ability to use suitable utensils to bring food/liquid to mouth and swallow
- Coding Scale: 01=Independent through 06=Total dependence, 07=Not attempted
- Assessment Period: Performance over 3-day assessment period

**GG0130B: Oral Hygiene**
- Intent: Ability to use suitable items to clean teeth, dentures, and mouth
- Assessment Method: Observation and demonstration preferred
- Documentation: Code patient's typical performance

**GG0170A: Roll Left and Right**
- Intent: Ability to roll from lying on back to left/right side and return
- Clinical Significance: Bed mobility and repositioning ability
- Safety Considerations: Fall risk and caregiver burden assessment

**GG0170C: Sit to Stand**
- Intent: Ability to come to standing position from sitting in chair or bed edge
- Functional Impact: Transfer ability and mobility independence
- Equipment Considerations: Use of assistive devices

### Section J: Health Conditions
**J1100: Pain Assessment**
- Intent: Identify if patient has pain and severity
- Coding: 0=No pain, 1=Pain present but not daily, 2=Daily pain
- Assessment Method: Patient self-report with clinical validation

### Section M: Skin Conditions
**M1311: Pressure Ulcer Assessment**
- Intent: Identify number of pressure ulcers at each stage
- Coding: A1=Stage 2 count, B1=Stage 3 count, C1=Stage 4 count, D1=Unstageable count
- Assessment Requirements: RN assessment with staging per NPUAP guidelines

## DISCIPLINE-SPECIFIC OASIS RESPONSIBILITIES

### Registered Nurse (RN) Items
- All M-items (diagnoses, medications, skin conditions)
- Health condition assessments (J-items)
- Administrative items (A-items)
- Cognitive and behavioral assessments (C-items)

### Physical Therapist (PT) Items
- Mobility functional items (GG0170 series)
- Balance and fall risk assessments
- Ambulation and locomotion items
- Equipment and safety assessments

### Occupational Therapist (OT) Items
- Self-care functional items (GG0130 series)
- Cognitive functional assessments
- Home safety and modification needs
- Adaptive equipment requirements

### Speech-Language Pathologist (SLP) Items
- Swallowing and nutritional items (K-items)
- Communication assessments
- Cognitive-communication evaluations
