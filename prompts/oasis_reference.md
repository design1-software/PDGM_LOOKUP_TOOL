# OASIS-E Assessment Reference - SOC/Recertification/Resumption/Significant Change

## CRITICAL MEDICARE COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK

**OASIS Assessment Timepoints per Medicare Guidelines:**
- **Start of Care (SOC)** - Initial comprehensive assessment
- **Recertification** - 60-day episode renewal assessment  
- **Resumption of Care** - After inpatient facility discharge
- **Significant Change in Condition** - When patient status materially changes

**ANTI-HALLUCINATION GUARDRAILS:**
- Use ONLY official CMS OASIS-E item numbers and terminology
- Reference ONLY verified clinical presentations from CMS guidance
- Include ONLY evidence-based assessment findings
- Demonstrate ONLY realistic skilled care justifications
- Never fabricate OASIS codes, response options, or clinical scenarios

## SOC ASSESSMENT EXAMPLE: J44.1 - COPD with Acute Exacerbation

### Administrative Information (Section A)

**M0100: This Assessment is Currently Being Completed for the Following Reason**
*Assessment: Start of Care - further visits planned*
*Coding: 1 = Start of care—further visits planned*
*Clinical Context: Patient discharged from hospital 2 days ago following COPD exacerbation treatment*
*Medicare Requirement: Comprehensive baseline assessment required within 5 days of SOC*

**M0102: Date of Physician Ordered Start of Care**
*Assessment: 01/15/2025*
*Clinical Context: Physician order date establishing home health eligibility*
*Documentation Requirement: Must align with 485 Plan of Care*

### Patient History and Diagnoses (Section B)

**M1021: Primary Diagnosis**
*Assessment: J44.1 - COPD with (acute) exacerbation*
*Clinical Rationale: Primary reason for home health services based on skilled need*
*Focus of Care: Respiratory status monitoring and medication management post-exacerbation*
*PDGM Impact: Determines PDGM grouping and reimbursement level*

**M1023: Other Diagnoses**
*Assessment: I10 - Essential hypertension; E11.9 - Type 2 diabetes mellitus without complications*
*Clinical Rationale: Comorbidities requiring monitoring and medication management*
*Care Planning Impact: Influences interdisciplinary care needs and safety considerations*

**M1028: Active Diagnoses - Symptom Control and Management**
*Assessment: J44.1 - COPD with acute exacerbation (1 = Yes)*
*Clinical Rationale: Requires ongoing symptom management and monitoring*
*Skilled Need Justification: RN assessment of respiratory status and medication effectiveness*

**M1033: Risk for Hospitalization**
*Assessment: 1 = Yes*
*Clinical Rationale: Recent hospitalization, respiratory instability, complex medication regimen*
*Risk Factors: COPD exacerbation history, medication adherence challenges, limited caregiver support*

### Living Arrangements (Section C)

**M1100: Patient Living Situation**
*Assessment: 01 = Patient lives alone*
*Clinical Context: 74-year-old widow living independently in single-story home*
*Safety Considerations: Limited emergency support, medication management concerns*
*Care Planning Impact: Increased need for caregiver education and emergency planning*

### Sensory Status (Section D)

**M1200: Vision**
*Assessment: 0 = Normal vision*
*Clinical Method: Snellen chart assessment and functional vision testing*
*Care Planning Impact: Able to read medication labels and educational materials*

**M1210: Ability to Hear**
*Assessment: 0 = Adequate*
*Clinical Method: Whisper test and functional hearing assessment*
*Care Planning Impact: Can receive verbal instructions and safety education*

### Integumentary Status (Section E)

**M1307: The Oldest Stage 2 Pressure Ulcer**
*Assessment: NA = No Stage 2 pressure ulcers*
*Clinical Method: Comprehensive skin assessment using NPUAP staging criteria*
*Documentation: Head-to-toe skin inspection with particular attention to bony prominences*

**M1311: Current Number of Unhealed Pressure Ulcers at Each Stage**
*Assessment: A1 = 0 (Stage 2); B1 = 0 (Stage 3); C1 = 0 (Stage 4); D1 = 0 (Unstageable)*
*Clinical Method: Complete skin assessment with staging per NPUAP guidelines*
*Risk Assessment: Mobility limitations due to dyspnea increase pressure ulcer risk*

### Respiratory Status (Section F)

**M1400: When is the Patient Dyspneic or Noticeably Short of Breath?**
*Assessment: 2 = When walking more than 20 feet, climbing stairs*
*Clinical Rationale: Consistent with COPD exacerbation and current functional limitations*
*Skilled Need: Requires respiratory assessment and activity tolerance monitoring*
*Homebound Justification: Dyspnea with minimal exertion limits community mobility*

### Cardiac Status (Section G)

**M1500: Symptoms in Heart Failure Patients**
*Assessment: NA = Not applicable (no heart failure diagnosis)*
*Clinical Method: Cardiovascular assessment including heart sounds, edema, JVD*
*Documentation: Rule out cardiac complications of COPD exacerbation*

### Elimination Status (Section H)

**M1600: Has this Patient had a Urinary Tract Infection in the Past 14 Days?**
*Assessment: 0 = No*
*Clinical Method: Review of recent medical records and urinalysis if indicated*
*Risk Factors: Steroid use may increase infection risk*

**M1610: Urinary Incontinence or Urinary Catheter Presence**
*Assessment: 0 = No incontinence or catheter*
*Clinical Method: Patient interview and functional assessment*
*Care Planning: Independent toileting supports homebound status*

### Neuro/Emotional/Behavioral Status (Section I)

**M1700: Cognitive Functioning**
*Assessment: 0 = Alert/oriented, able to focus and shift attention, comprehends and recalls task directions independently*
*Clinical Method: Mini-Mental State Exam and functional cognitive assessment*
*Care Planning Impact: Able to participate in medication management and self-care education*

**M1710: When Confused**
*Assessment: NA = Never*
*Clinical Rationale: Consistent with cognitive functioning assessment*
*Medication Safety: Able to manage complex medication regimen independently*

**M1720: When Anxious**
*Assessment: 1 = Less than daily*
*Clinical Context: Mild anxiety related to breathing difficulties and recent hospitalization*
*Intervention Need: Education on COPD management and breathing techniques*

### ADL/IADLs (Section J - GG Items)

**GG0130A: Self-Care - Eating**
*Assessment: 01 = Independent*
*Clinical Method: Observation during assessment visit*
*Functional Context: No swallowing difficulties, maintains adequate nutrition*

**GG0130B: Self-Care - Oral Hygiene**
*Assessment: 01 = Independent*
*Clinical Method: Patient demonstration and self-report*
*Care Planning: Maintains oral health independently*

**GG0130C: Self-Care - Toileting Hygiene**
*Assessment: 02 = Setup or clean-up assistance*
*Clinical Rationale: Dyspnea with exertion requires pacing and energy conservation*
*Skilled Need: OT evaluation for adaptive techniques and equipment*

**GG0170A: Mobility - Roll Left and Right**
*Assessment: 01 = Independent*
*Clinical Method: Bed mobility demonstration*
*Safety Assessment: No fall risk with bed mobility*

**GG0170C: Mobility - Sit to Stand**
*Assessment: 03 = Supervision or touching assistance*
*Clinical Rationale: Dyspnea and fatigue require standby assistance for safety*
*Equipment Need: May benefit from raised toilet seat or grab bars*

### Medications (Section K)

**M2001: Drug Regimen Review**
*Assessment: 1 = Yes, medication issues found and addressed*
*Clinical Findings: Inhaler technique needs improvement, steroid taper education required*
*Skilled Need: RN medication reconciliation and patient education*

**M2003: Medication Follow-up**
*Assessment: 1 = Yes, physician contacted or medication therapy changed*
*Clinical Action: Contacted physician regarding inhaler technique and steroid side effects*
*Documentation: Communication with physician documented in clinical record*

### Care Management (Section L)

**M2102: Types and Sources of Assistance**
*Assessment: Assistance needed with: 1 = ADL assistance; 3 = Medication administration/management*
*Clinical Rationale: Requires assistance with energy-intensive ADLs and complex medication regimen*
*Caregiver Assessment: Limited informal support system*

### Therapy Need and Plan of Care (Section M)

**M2200: Therapy Need**
*Assessment: 1 = Yes, patient meets criteria for therapy services*
*Clinical Rationale: Functional limitations due to dyspnea and deconditioning*
*Therapy Goals: Improve activity tolerance and energy conservation techniques*
