# Documentation Roadmap Reference Examples

## Example: J44.1 - COPD with Acute Exacerbation

### Good OASIS Documentation Example
"COPD exacerbation: Increased SOB, wheezing, SpO2 88% on room air, productive cough with yellow sputum, started on nebulizer treatments and oral steroids post-hospital discharge. Requires skilled nursing for respiratory status monitoring, medication management, and caregiver teaching on inhaler technique and exacerbation recognition."

### Poor OASIS Documentation Example
"Patient has COPD, uses inhalers, no current symptoms reported."

### RN/LPN Focus
- Assess breath sounds and respiratory effort at each visit
- Monitor SpO2 and oxygen requirements
- Teach proper inhaler/nebulizer technique
- Medication reconciliation and adherence monitoring
- Educate on exacerbation warning signs
- Reinforce smoking cessation if applicable

### PT/OT Focus
- Assess endurance and activity tolerance
- Energy conservation techniques
- Fall risk assessment due to dyspnea
- Safe ambulation with oxygen equipment
- Breathing techniques during activity

### ST Focus
- Breathing control for speech production
- Safe swallowing assessment if applicable
- Communication strategies during dyspnea episodes

### HHA Focus
- Assist with ADLs while monitoring for SOB
- Observe for increased wheezing or respiratory distress
- Report changes in sputum color or amount
- Reinforce safe activity levels

### Clinical Validation Checklist
- Document specific respiratory symptoms (SOB, wheezing, cough)
- Record SpO2 readings and oxygen requirements
- Note recent medication changes or hospitalizations
- Justify homebound status due to dyspnea with minimal exertion
- Link skilled need to respiratory monitoring and medication management

## Example: I50.21 - Acute Systolic Heart Failure

### Good OASIS Documentation Example
"CHF exacerbation: Increased lower extremity edema, weight gain of 6 pounds in 3 days, SOB at rest and with minimal exertion, JVD present. Lasix dose increased, dietary sodium restriction initiated. Requires skilled nursing for daily weight monitoring, fluid status assessment, and medication management."

### Poor OASIS Documentation Example
"CHF patient, some swelling noted, taking heart medications."

### RN/LPN Focus
- Daily weight monitoring and trending
- Assess for edema progression
- Monitor lung sounds for crackles
- Medication teaching and adherence
- Fluid restriction education
- Signs/symptoms of worsening CHF

### PT/OT Focus
- Activity tolerance assessment
- Monitor for exertional dyspnea
- Energy conservation and pacing techniques
- Safe mobility with fluid restrictions

### ST Focus
- Cognitive assessment for medication management
- Swallowing evaluation if applicable

### HHA Focus
- Assist with daily weights
- Monitor fluid intake and output
- Report sudden weight gain (>2 lbs in 24 hours)
- Assist with low-sodium meal preparation

### Clinical Validation Checklist
- Document specific CHF symptoms (edema, weight gain, dyspnea)
- Record daily weights and fluid balance
- Note medication adjustments
- Justify skilled need for monitoring and medication management
- Document homebound status due to cardiac limitations
